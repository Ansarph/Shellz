Shared hosting plans

The Power Plan is WebHostingPad's all-inclusive shared option. It advertises free SSL, SiteLock Lite, a website builder, one-click WordPress, email accounts, and support for multiple sites and domains. For a basic blog, portfolio, or small business site, that covers most common needs.

Power Plan Plus is positioned as a WordPress-optimized step up. It adds higher-power servers, automated and on-demand backups, stronger SSL options, and priority support. Buyers who expect to publish frequently should compare it with the dedicated WordPress tiers.

WordPress hosting

WebHostingPad's WordPress line includes Basic, Pro, and Premium tiers. Its official page lists free SSL, automated and anytime backups, malware scanning and removal, free WordPress transfer, priority support, CDN access, and increasing memory or computing power depending on the plan.

WordPress Basic

Suitable for a new blog or small affiliate site that needs backups, SSL, migration assistance, and malware tools.

WordPress Pro

Offers more computing power for larger content sites, heavier themes, or additional plugins.

WordPress Premium

The highest published WordPress tier, aimed at sites needing more memory and processing capacity.

cPanel costs extra

WebHostingPad's published terms state that accounts hosted with cPanel carry an additional monthly charge. Ask which control panel comes with your selected plan and confirm the total if cPanel is important to you.

Understanding “unlimited” resources

WebHostingPad uses “unlimited” language for hosting space, bandwidth, websites, databases, and email. This does not mean unrestricted use for file archives, backup storage, large media repositories, or resource-heavy services. Shared hosting remains subject to fair-use and server-resource policies.

For a normal blog, portfolio, or small business site, those restrictions may never become a problem. They matter more for video files, downloadable archives, large mailboxes, or high-traffic applications.

Email and website builders

The provider advertises domain-based email, webmail, POP3/IMAP support, forwarding, autoresponders, mailing lists, and spam filtering. This can save a small business from buying a separate email package at launch.

Website-building options include WordPress, Weebly, and other site-builder tools. Weebly may suit a simple brochure site, while WordPress is better for long-term content growth, plugins, blogging, and customization.

Backups and security

Power Plan Plus and WordPress plans emphasize automated or on-demand backups. WordPress plans also advertise malware scanning and removal, SSL, and CDN tools. Before buying, confirm backup frequency, retention, restore procedures, and possible restoration fees.

Hosting security does not replace basic website maintenance. Keep WordPress, themes, and plugins updated, use unique passwords, enable two-factor authentication when available, and maintain an independent backup for important sites.

Support

WebHostingPad's support center lists ticket support, a knowledge base, and phone support for eligible plans, with technical agents described as available 24/7. Phone support is not applicable to the Mini Plan.

No independent Shellz support benchmark is included on this page. Buyers should verify migration help, SSL setup, backup restoration, email configuration, billing questions, and cancellation handling before moving an important site.

Refund policy

Eligible new shared-hosting purchases include a 30-day money-back period. Official terms exclude renewals, domains, add-ons, outside SSL purchases, site-transfer fees, service fees, and accounts violating the terms. Think of it as a hosting refund rather than a refund of every checkout item.

Final verdict

WebHostingPad is a legitimate budget option for small websites and beginner WordPress projects. Its strongest value comes from low introductory prices, SSL, domain email, builders, WordPress tools, and the eligible first-year domain offer.

Choose it when the site is small, price matters, and you are willing to read the billing and fair-use details. Compare another provider if you need premium managed WordPress support, fixed resources, enterprise performance, or cPanel included at no additional cost.
