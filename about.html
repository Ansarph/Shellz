About Shellz

Shellz helps beginners and small businesses compare hosting, domain registrars, website builders, and coupon deals with plain-language buyer checks, renewal-price warnings, and transparent testing status.

Why Shellz exists

Shellz was created for people who are ready to buy hosting but do not want to be surprised by renewal prices, checkout add-ons, confusing plan limits, or expired coupon codes. The goal is simple: explain what a beginner should check before paying, then point readers to the most relevant review, comparison, coupon, or setup guide.

Most hosting pages online focus on the lowest advertised price. Shellz focuses on the real buying decision: the first bill, the renewal bill, the exact plan limits, support scope, backup rules, domain/email costs, refund terms, and whether the provider is actually a good fit for the website being built.

Who writes and reviews Shellz content

Shellz is reviewed by Ansar P.H., who focuses on practical hosting, domain, coupon, and website-building decisions for beginners and small business owners. Pages are written to be useful before checkout, not just to describe provider features.

Buyer-first angle

Each review asks whether the provider fits a real project: beginner website, WordPress blog, small business site, WooCommerce store, VPS project, domain purchase, or budget hosting plan.

Renewal-price focus

Shellz highlights the difference between the first promotional price and the later renewal price because the second bill is often the number that matters most.

Coupon honesty

Coupon pages separate typed codes, auto-applied offers, voucher-style deals, and normal public promotions. Readers are asked to verify the final checkout total before payment.

How Shellz reviews hosting providers

Shellz pages use official provider information, plan comparisons, coupon/deal checks, renewal warnings, and buyer-fit notes. When first-hand performance data is not yet published, the page says so clearly instead of pretending a lab test has been completed.

Testing and proof policy

Shellz is moving toward stronger first-hand review evidence. The target proof set for major reviews includes checkout screenshots, renewal-price screenshots, dashboard notes, uptime monitoring, PageSpeed or GTmetrix checks, TTFB checks, and support-response notes. Until those items are visible on a page, Shellz labels the review as an official-source buyer checklist rather than a completed hands-on benchmark.

Affiliate disclosure and independence

Some Shellz links may be affiliate links, which means Shellz may earn a commission if a reader buys through them at no extra cost to the reader. Affiliate links do not remove renewal warnings, plan-limit notes, coupon verification notes, or negative points. Shellz may recommend comparing alternatives when a provider is not the best fit.

Read more in the review methodology, editorial policy, affiliate disclosure, and corrections policy.

Corrections and contact

Hosting prices, coupons, plan limits, and renewal terms can change quickly. If you find an outdated price, broken coupon, provider change, or wording issue, contact Shellz so the page can be reviewed and corrected.
