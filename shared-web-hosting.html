Shared web hosting is usually the first paid hosting plan people buy. It is affordable, easy to set up, and good enough for many blogs, portfolios, small business pages, and beginner WordPress sites.

The problem is that shared hosting is often advertised with very low first-term prices. The real buying decision should include renewal cost, email, backups, SSL, storage, support, site limits, and what happens when your site grows.

Best forNew websites, blogs, portfolios, small local business sites, and first WordPress projects.
Avoid ifYou expect heavy traffic, large ecommerce checkout activity, custom server software, or mission-critical uptime.

Why shared hosting is still useful

Shared hosting works because many small websites do not need dedicated resources. If your site has a few pages, a blog, contact forms, and normal traffic, a good shared plan can be the cheapest practical starting point.

It also removes many technical decisions. Most shared hosts include a control panel, SSL tools, one-click WordPress installation, basic email options, and support for common beginner questions.

What to check before buying a cheap plan

Check how long the introductory price lasts, what the renewal price becomes, whether the free domain renews at a higher cost, and whether backups are included or paid. These details decide the real cost.

Also review storage type, inode limits, number of websites, email mailbox limits, migration policy, malware tools, and support channels. Cheap hosting can be good, but only when the limits match your site.

When to upgrade from shared hosting

Upgrade when your site becomes slow even after basic optimization, when ecommerce checkout matters, when you need staging and better backups, or when support suggests you are hitting resource limits.

A good upgrade path may be managed WordPress hosting, VPS hosting, cloud hosting, or ecommerce hosting depending on the project.

Shellz recommendation

For a first website, shared hosting is a smart start if you choose it with realistic expectations. Do not overbuy on day one, but do not ignore renewal pricing.

Pick a plan you can understand, publish the site, track performance, and upgrade only when your traffic or income justifies it.

Quick answers

Frequently asked questions

Is shared hosting good for WordPress?

Yes, for small WordPress sites. For busy blogs, WooCommerce, or client sites, managed WordPress or VPS hosting may be better.

Why is shared hosting cheap?

Many websites share the same server resources. This lowers cost but also creates limits on CPU, memory, and performance during traffic spikes.

What is the biggest shared hosting trap?

Renewal pricing and paid add-ons. The first bill may look low, but the second term can cost much more.
