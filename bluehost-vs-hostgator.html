Editor note

My quick answer

Bluehost is the better first click for most readers comparing these two options.

For most first-time website owners, I would start with Bluehost because the path feels more WordPress-first and less coupon-driven. HostGator can still be useful if you are mainly chasing a cheap shared-hosting deal, but you need to watch the coupon, billing term, and renewal total carefully.

Important: this page is intentionally honest about proof. Shellz has not added checkout screenshots, renewal screenshots, speed-test screenshots, or uptime data for this comparison yet. Those are marked as pending instead of being claimed.

How I would think about this decision

This is not a speed-test claim. Shellz has not added live benchmark screenshots for this comparison yet. The goal here is simpler: help a beginner avoid picking a plan only because the first price looks low.

Both brands can work for a basic site. The real difference is how comfortable you feel during setup, what the checkout adds to the cart, and what the plan renews at after the intro period.
