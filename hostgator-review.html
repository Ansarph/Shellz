Price and renewal

The first bill and the second bill are two different buying decisions

Shellz does not treat a promotional monthly number as the long-term cost. Before paying for HostGator, save the plan name, billing term, full amount due today, renewal amount, renewal billing cycle, domain renewal, email cost or limits, backup rules, and optional add-ons.

  • 1. First totalRecord the actual amount charged today, not only the advertised monthly equivalent.
  • 2. Renewal amountCheck the price for the same plan after the promotional or first billing period.
  • 3. Included servicesSeparate domain, email, backups, security, migration, and premium support from the hosting price.
  • 4. Exit costKnow the refund terms, migration path, and what happens to a bundled domain or email service if you leave.

Support scope

Ask what support covers before you need urgent help

Support should be evaluated by channel, scope, and response experience. Shellz has not yet published a timed HostGator support test. Confirm whether the issue you care about is covered by hosting support or is a WordPress/plugin/code problem that may require a developer.

Hosting-platform problem

Ask whether account, server, DNS, SSL, backup, or platform issues are within normal support scope.

WordPress or application problem

Check whether plugins, themes, custom code, database errors, or application debugging are included or excluded.

Response expectations

Do not infer response quality from a marketing badge. Check the current channels and look for published Shellz evidence when available.

Pros and trade-offs

HostGator pros and cons for a real buyer

Pros

  • Straightforward shared-hosting comparison for beginners.
  • Familiar hosting workflow can reduce migration friction for some users.
  • Useful comparison against Bluehost and Hostinger for small sites.

Trade-offs

  • Renewal price needs a separate long-term budget check.
  • Coupon eligibility and plan rules can change, so a code should not decide the purchase.
  • Growing stores or applications may outgrow the shared-hosting route.
