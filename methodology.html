Methodology

How Shellz reviews web hosting providers

A transparent scoring framework helps visitors understand why a provider is recommended and what still needs to be checked before purchase.

What Shellz focuses on: Shellz checks hosting coupons, checkout totals, selected extras, and later-price information before payment — then publishes the screenshots and explains what they do and do not prove.
Affiliate disclosure: Some provider buttons may be affiliate links. Shellz may earn a commission if you buy through them, at no extra cost to you. We recommend checking live pricing, renewal terms, plan limits, and refund rules before purchasing.

First-bill and renewal method

How Shellz records hosting checkout costs before payment

For the Checkout Cost Tests hub, Shellz records the exact plan or configuration visible on screen, the billing term, introductory price, selected extra services, amount due before payment, and renewal or next-charge details when the provider displays them.

Record the selected cart

We note the plan, configuration, term, data center or control-panel choice when visible.

Separate price layers

We keep the introductory price, coupon line, wider cart savings, selected extras, tax, and final total separate instead of combining them into one claim.

Capture the later charge

When the checkout shows a renewal price or next charge, we record that exact figure. When it does not, the page says the numerical renewal amount was not shown.

Scoring categories

Value and pricing transparency

We look at intro price, renewal price, included essentials, plan limits, refund window, and whether important features require add-ons.

Beginner experience

We consider onboarding, dashboard usability, WordPress setup, migration help, support access, and documentation quality.

Security and backups

We check SSL, backups, restore options, malware tools, account security, and whether features are included or upsold.

Performance and growth path

We compare caching, CDN, modern storage, upgrade paths, and whether the host can support a growing website.

Important disclosure

Shellz may earn affiliate commissions from some links. Affiliate income should never decide the final ranking alone. The goal is to help readers choose the host that fits their website and budget.

How Shellz documents checkout verification

When Shellz has first-hand checkout evidence, we record the code or deal shown, the date of the check, the visible checkout message and totals, and whether payment was completed. We publish the screenshot with a limitation note so visitors can separate what the captured cart proves from what still needs to be confirmed in their own checkout.

How Shellz checks hosting checkout add-ons and renewal prices

For a standard hosting checkout check, Shellz records the selected plan, billing term, first-invoice price, selected extras, subtotal, tax or VAT where visible, final total, and any renewal or auto-renew wording shown before payment. When an optional service is selected, Shellz reports that state explicitly instead of implying the host added it to every cart.

Testing roadmap

How Shellz will mark a host as fully tested

Shellz does not mark a hosting provider as fully tested until the proof exists. Until then, reviews show “evidence not yet published” rather than unsupported claims.

  • Checkout proof: final cart total, billing term, coupon behavior, and visible add-ons.
  • Renewal proof: renewal price confirmation or provider documentation showing future price changes.
  • Speed proof: repeatable speed/TTFB/Core Web Vitals checks using a comparable test site.
  • Uptime proof: monitoring data collected over time rather than one-time claims.
  • Support proof: response time and answer quality for beginner-level questions.
  • Dashboard proof: screenshots or notes showing control panel, backups, SSL, email, and migration options.
