Methodology

How Shellz reviews web hosting providers

Shellz uses buyer-fit research and evidence status instead of implying every provider has been hands-on benchmarked. Public plan research, checkout proof, and performance tests are labelled separately.

Affiliate disclosure: Some provider buttons may be affiliate links. Shellz may earn a commission if you buy through them, at no extra cost to you. We recommend checking live pricing, renewal terms, plan limits, and refund rules before purchasing.

Evidence note: Shellz separates published checkout or coupon checks from tests still in progress. A provider is not described as speed-tested, uptime-tested, or support-tested unless the supporting log, screenshot, or dated note is published.

Buyer-fit research categories

Value and pricing transparency

We look at intro price, renewal price, included essentials, plan limits, refund window, and whether important features require add-ons.

Beginner experience

We consider onboarding, dashboard usability, WordPress setup, migration help, support access, and documentation quality.

Security and backups

We check SSL, backups, restore options, malware tools, account security, and whether features are included or upsold.

Performance and growth path

We compare caching, CDN, modern storage, upgrade paths, and whether the host can support a growing website.

Important disclosure

Shellz may earn affiliate commissions from some links. Affiliate income should never decide the final ranking alone. The goal is to help readers choose the host that fits their website and budget.

Evidence that strengthens a review

Checkout captures, exact renewal documentation, comparable speed tests, uptime logs, dashboard screenshots, and dated support-response notes can strengthen a review. Shellz does not claim those tests are completed until the evidence is published.

Testing roadmap

How Shellz will mark a host as fully tested

Shellz does not mark a hosting provider as fully tested until the proof exists. Until then, reviews show “evidence not yet published” rather than unsupported claims.

  • Checkout proof: final cart total, billing term, coupon behavior, and visible add-ons.
  • Renewal proof: renewal price confirmation or provider documentation showing future price changes.
  • Speed proof: repeatable speed/TTFB/Core Web Vitals checks using a comparable test site.
  • Uptime proof: monitoring data collected over time rather than one-time claims.
  • Support proof: response time and answer quality for beginner-level questions.
  • Dashboard proof: screenshots or notes showing control panel, backups, SSL, email, and migration options.
