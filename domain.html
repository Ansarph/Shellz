How to choose a domain name

A good domain is easy to say, easy to spell, and not confusing when spoken aloud. Short is helpful, but clarity is more important than being clever.

Avoid hyphen-heavy names, trademark risk, hard spellings, and domains that only make sense for one narrow idea if you plan to expand later.

Registrar features that matter

A good registrar should make DNS easy, show renewal pricing clearly, support domain privacy where allowed, provide account security features, and make transfers understandable.

If you plan to use professional email, check email forwarding, DNS records, and whether the registrar pushes unnecessary add-ons during checkout.

The domain renewal trap

Many domain buyers focus on the first-year price and ignore renewal. Some extensions are cheap to register but expensive to renew. Premium domains can also have special pricing.

Before checkout, write down the renewal price, privacy cost, email cost, and transfer policy. This prevents surprises later.

Shellz recommendation

Choose a reliable registrar, keep the domain separate from risky experiments, enable account security, and save renewal reminders.

For serious projects, your domain is more important than the hosting plan. You can change hosting later, but losing a domain can damage the brand.

Quick answers

Frequently asked questions

Should I buy domain and hosting from the same company?

It is convenient, but not required. Keeping the domain separate can give more control if you move hosting later.

What is domain privacy?

Domain privacy hides some public ownership contact details where registry rules allow it. Availability depends on the extension and registrar.

What is the biggest domain mistake?

Ignoring renewal price, transfer rules, and account security.
