I've spent the last several years deep in web hosting comparisons - the kind of work where you read fifty pages of fine print so other people do not have to. So when "AI website builder" started showing up in every other ad, my first reaction was not excitement. It was suspicion. I have seen too many hosting companies bolt "AI-powered" onto an old template generator and call it innovation.

I spent three weeks building the same basic business site - a fictional local bakery, since it is specific enough to test real features but simple enough to be fair to every tool - on six different AI website builders. Same brief, same content, same goal: get a usable site live and see what breaks.

Before you compare tools

The thing nobody tells beginners upfront

Type one: hosted all-in-one AI builders

Wix, Hostinger, Durable, and similar platforms generate your site and also host it, manage your domain flow, and keep the website inside their platform.

This is usually easier for a true beginner with a simple local business or portfolio. The trade-off is lock-in. If you leave later, you may need to rebuild rather than migrate.

Type two: code-generating or advanced design tools

Framer, v0, Lovable, and similar tools feel closer to "vibe coding" or AI-assisted design than traditional website building.

Some can give you a better path to real code or more complex future projects, but the learning curve is higher and the lock-in rules still need checking.

Hands-on notes

What happened with each AI website builder

Wix: the safest choice, and I mean that as a compliment

Wix's newer AI flow asks a series of business questions, then generates a real multi-page site rather than just a homepage mockup. The bakery site came back with five pages, working navigation, a contact form, and copy that was generic but not embarrassing.

Setup took about 8 minutes including the AI questions. The custom order form was straightforward with the drag-and-drop form builder.

Best for: beginners who want the lowest chance of breaking something.

Try Wix →

Hostinger AI: the budget pick, with a real renewal catch

Hostinger's AI builder is impressive for the price. It generates a workable site quickly with AI copy and stock images. The catch is the same one Shellz often flags in hosting: promotional pricing is much lower than renewal pricing.

The custom order form was rougher than Wix. A basic form was fine, but the multi-category structure needed manual rebuilding in the editor.

Best for: tight budgets where the renewal price is understood before payment.

Durable: fastest setup, shallowest result

Durable generated a complete site in under 30 seconds. For someone who wants a simple business presence online today, that speed is real value.

The trade-off is depth. The site felt more like a clean simple presence page than a flexible multi-page business system, and the custom form requirement exposed the limits quickly.

Best for: local service businesses that need basic contact information online quickly.

Try Durable →

Framer: the design-lover's tool with a learning curve

Framer produced the most visually polished output in the test. The generated bakery site looked like something a designer might actually charge for.

The challenge came after generation. Customizing beyond the first draft pushed into Framer's design-tool interface, which expects more comfort with layout and component thinking.

Best for: beginners who care more about visual quality than a no-friction first day.

Try Framer →

10Web: choose it if WordPress matters

10Web's pitch is that it is WordPress under the hood, with AI doing the initial generation. That matters if you care about long-term flexibility, SEO control, plugins, and a clearer migration path.

The bakery site came out reasonably well. The custom order form required a plugin, which is normal in WordPress but less automatic than a beginner might expect.

Best for: people who want AI help without giving up WordPress flexibility.

Try 10Web →

Squarespace: polished, but the AI was not the main reason to choose it

Squarespace has a strong design reputation, and the output was clean, on-brand, and professional-looking. But the AI generation felt more like guided template selection than a truly intelligent site build.

It is still a solid choice if you already like the Squarespace design system. Just do not choose it only because the sales page says AI.

Best for: people who already want Squarespace's design aesthetic.

Try Squarespace →

The SEO problem almost nobody mentions clearly

A beautiful AI-generated website can still struggle in Google if the pages are thin, the structure is weak, or the platform outputs a single-page app style site that does not expose content cleanly. "Includes SEO tools" usually means basic meta fields, not a real content or local SEO strategy.

In this test, Wix, 10Web, Squarespace, and Framer were the safer choices for crawlable output. Durable and Hostinger can still work for search, but beginners should add real page depth, service details, local information, related links, and original content before expecting rankings.

Final verdict

What I would actually tell a beginner

Choose based on the problem, not the AI label

If you want the safest all-around choice and do not mind paying a bit more, choose Wix. If budget is the deciding factor, choose Hostinger only after comparing the first bill with the renewal price. If you need something live today and the site is genuinely simple, Durable is fast.

If design quality matters most and you can spend a weekend learning, Framer is strong. If you have been told you need WordPress specifically, 10Web is the better AI-assisted route.

The question to ask before you buy

Do not ask only, "Which AI website builder is best?" Ask: "What happens to my site if I want to leave this platform in two years?"

That single question eliminates more bad fits than most feature tables. Wix, Durable, and Squarespace are convenient but more locked in. 10Web gives a clearer WordPress migration path. Framer can offer more advanced flexibility, but beginners must understand the workflow first.

Quick answers

AI website builder FAQ

Which AI website builder is best for beginners?

Wix is the safest all-around choice in this test because it generated a complete multi-page site, handled navigation and forms well, and gave enough editing control for a beginner.

Which AI website builder is best for a tight budget?

Hostinger AI is the budget pick, but you should compare the promotional price with the renewal price before committing your website and time to the platform.

Which AI website builder is best if I want WordPress?

10Web is the best fit from this shortlist if WordPress matters because it uses WordPress under the hood and gives a clearer long-term migration path than fully locked hosted builders.

Should I publish the AI-generated site immediately?

No. Check copy accuracy, mobile layout, contact forms, SEO titles, local details, images, legal pages, calls to action, renewal costs, and domain/email terms before publishing.
