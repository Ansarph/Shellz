Editor note

My quick answer

Hostinger is the better first click for most readers comparing these two options.

Hostinger is the safer path for less technical VPS beginners. Contabo can be attractive for technical users chasing server resources and dedicated/VPS value, but you must be more careful with setup, management, location, and support expectations.

Important: this page is intentionally honest about proof. Shellz has not added checkout screenshots, renewal screenshots, speed-test screenshots, or uptime data for this comparison yet. Those are marked as pending instead of being claimed.

How I would think about this decision

VPS hosting is not the same as beginner shared hosting. You may need to understand server resources, operating systems, security, backups, updates, and what support will or will not do.

Contabo often attracts technical buyers looking for strong resource value. Hostinger is usually easier to explain to a beginner who wants a guided VPS-style product.
