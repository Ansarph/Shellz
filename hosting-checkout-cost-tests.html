How we record a checkout

Evidence boundaries

What these checkout screenshots can and cannot prove

What the evidence can show

  • The selected product or configuration visible in the captured checkout.
  • The term, price, discount, selected extra, total, renewal, or next-charge information visible on screen.
  • The date on which Shellz performed the documented check.

What the evidence cannot guarantee

  • The same price for every country, account, plan, term, tax situation, or future checkout.
  • That an introductory price applies to renewal unless the screen explicitly says so.
  • Speed, uptime, support quality, or long-term account experience.

Always check your own cart: remove services you do not want, confirm the billing term, note the renewal or next-charge amount, and verify the final total before payment.

Frequently asked questions

Checkout cost test FAQ

Are these completed hosting purchases?

No. The documented checkout checks stopped before payment. The screenshots record the information visible before payment.

Does a screenshot guarantee I will see the same price?

No. The captured result can vary by plan, term, account, country, tax treatment, selected services, and provider campaign.

Why compare the first bill with renewal or the next charge?

A low introductory payment can be temporary. The renewal or next-charge line gives better long-term budgeting context when it is visible.

Are selected extras the same as hidden fees?

No. Shellz does not call a clearly displayed selected service a hidden fee. The SiteGround example shows why buyers should review selected extra services before paying.
