Editor note

My quick answer

WPX Hosting is the better first click for most readers comparing these two options.

WPX is the more realistic first choice for many smaller WordPress sites that want managed hosting without immediately jumping into premium-agency pricing. Kinsta is still attractive for businesses that already know they need a high-end managed WordPress platform and can justify the cost.

Important: this page is intentionally honest about proof. Shellz has not added checkout screenshots, renewal screenshots, speed-test screenshots, or uptime data for this comparison yet. Those are marked as not yet published instead of being claimed.

How I would think about this decision

This comparison is mainly about buyer fit. A small blog, local business site, or affiliate site usually has a different budget than an agency, SaaS company, or high-traffic publication.

Do not choose either one only because of a discount. Managed WordPress hosting only makes sense when the support level, backups, staging, performance tools, and renewal cost match the project.
