Step 1: Decide what your website is for

Before buying anything, decide the goal of the site. A personal blog, business website, portfolio, coupon site, ecommerce store, and affiliate review site need different tools.

  • Simple business site: domain, hosting, contact page, service pages, and basic SEO.
  • Blog or review site: WordPress hosting, article templates, categories, and related links.
  • Coupon or affiliate site: hosting, coupon pages, affiliate disclosure, tracking, and a corrections policy.
  • Store: WooCommerce or ecommerce hosting, payment setup, SSL, backups, and security tools.

Step 2: Buy the domain name carefully

Your domain is your website address. For most beginners, a short .com name is easier to remember. Avoid long names, confusing spellings, trademark problems, and hyphens unless you have a clear reason.

Before checkout, check renewal price, WHOIS/privacy cost, email forwarding options, and transfer rules. A cheap first-year domain can become more expensive at renewal.

Step 3: Choose hosting based on your project

For a first website, cheap shared hosting or beginner WordPress hosting is usually enough. For a serious WordPress, WooCommerce, agency, or high-traffic project, managed WordPress or managed cloud hosting may be better.

Step 4: Understand email separately

Email is often separate from hosting. Some hosts include basic email, some charge extra, and some users prefer Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Zoho, or domain email forwarding. For a full explanation, read domain vs hosting vs email.

Step 5: Build your first pages

Start with the pages that help users and search engines understand your site. A small website should usually have Home, About, Contact, Privacy, Terms, and at least one useful guide or service page.

Step 6: Use coupons carefully

Hosting coupons can save money, but the real price is the renewal cost and add-ons. Check whether the coupon applies to monthly, annual, or multi-year billing. Some deals are auto-applied and do not have a public coupon code.

Use the Shellz hosting coupon hub to compare available coupon pages and deal notes.

Step 7: Publish and index

After publishing, connect Google Search Console, submit your sitemap, and request indexing for your most important pages. Then build related links from related articles so Google can discover your site structure.
