How to read backup claims

Frequency, retention and restoration are different protections

Backup frequency

Frequency tells you how often a copy is created. A daily backup is more useful than a weekly copy for a store, membership site or frequently edited WordPress site.

Retention period

Retention tells you how far back you can go. Daily copies are less useful when only the latest copy remains after malware or an unnoticed error has existed for several days.

Restore access

A backup is not fully useful until you know whether you can restore files, databases, email or the whole account yourself—and whether support or restore fees are involved.

Hidden backup costs

Six questions to ask before checkout

Is backup included on this exact plan?

Do not assume the provider's premium-plan backup feature also applies to its cheapest plan.

How many restore points remain?

Ask for the number of daily, weekly and monthly copies—not only whether backups are “automatic.”

Can you restore without support?

A one-click restore can be faster than opening a ticket during a website emergency.

Is restoration charged separately?

Some systems include backup creation but restrict or charge for restore requests.

What is excluded?

Confirm whether email, databases, staging sites, external storage and large files are included.

What happens after cancellation?

Hosting backups may disappear when the account closes. Download an independent copy before cancellation or migration.

Shellz recommendation: keep an independent backup

Host-level backups are useful, but they should not be your only copy. Keep an independent backup under your control—such as encrypted cloud storage or a local copy—and periodically test that the files and database can actually be restored.

Buyer fit

Which backup setup should you look for?

Simple blog or portfolio

Weekly backups may be acceptable when content changes rarely, but daily backups and an independent WordPress copy reduce risk.

Business or active WordPress site

Prefer daily backups, several restore points and a self-service restore option. Confirm databases and email are covered.

WooCommerce or membership site

Daily backups may still lose recent orders or member activity. Compare real-time or more frequent application-level backup options in addition to hosting backups.
