How I review a hosting page

  1. Review the public plan page.Record the exact plan name, advertised monthly equivalent, billing term, domain and SSL rules, email, backups, and visible limits.
  2. Check renewal information separately.Record the renewal amount and renewal term when the provider publishes them. If the exact renewal cannot be confirmed, label it unclear instead of guessing.
  3. Classify the coupon or deal.Public codes, auto-applied deals, no-code offers, and checkout-only promos are labelled differently. Unconfirmed offers are not presented as guaranteed savings.
  4. Separate checkout proof from checkout advice.A page may tell readers what to inspect at checkout without claiming Shellz captured the cart. A checkout test is only called published when the page shows or describes the dated evidence.
  5. Separate performance proof.Speed, uptime, dashboard, and support claims are not treated as independently tested unless the supporting result is published.
  6. Write the second-bill warning clearly.The first-term price and the renewal amount are treated as separate buying decisions. Exact plan and term context matter.

What I check before recommending a provider

  • Whether the offer is a public code, auto-applied deal, no-code deal, or unconfirmed claim.
  • Whether renewal price, setup fee, location fee, email, domain, backups, and plan limits can change the total cost.
  • Whether the provider is better for beginners, WordPress users, domain buyers, VPS users, or managed hosting buyers.
  • Whether a page needs stronger proof such as checkout screenshots, uptime data, speed tests, or support screenshots.
